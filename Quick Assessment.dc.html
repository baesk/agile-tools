Priority ranking

Step 1

The four value factors

Reach, Impact and Urgency are each rated High (3), Medium (2), Low (1) or N/A (0) and added together (0–9). Risk is then applied as a percentage modifier — High 100%, Medium 80%, Low 50% — so lower-risk work is discounted rather than inflated.

Reach — usage, efficiency, experience

High · frequent, high-volume use with major gains

Medium · moderate use and reasonable gains

Low · occasional use with minor gains

Impact — breadth of benefit

High · all or the majority of a cohort benefits

Medium · several groups, or one large group

Low · a single or small group benefits

Urgency — timing pressure

High · directive, capital project or immovable deadline

Medium · no deadline, but as soon as practical

Low · no timeline and no defined deadline

Risk — percentage modifier

High · 100% · many failures, major impact or security risk

Medium · 80% · some failures, manageable with a workaround

Low · 50% · almost no failures, minor annoyances only

Step 2

Combined value = (Reach + Impact + Urgency) × Risk

Reach, Impact and Urgency (each 0–3) are added, then multiplied by the Risk modifier (High 100%, Medium 80%, Low 50%) — giving a total of 0–9 that maps to a single Combined value of 3, 5, 8, 13 or 20 using the bands below. The highlighted row is this backlog item.

How the effort size is chosen

Complexity is not a separate number — the story point already captures it. The three effort answers (task knowledge, dependencies and work effort) are averaged into a size, rounding up to the larger size when the average falls between two. Items that land on XL (8 pts) should be split into smaller stories, and XXL (13 pts) items must be split before they are committed to a sprint. See the Effort guide tab for the full cheat sheet.
